Methodology

The WWAI is calculated hourly from WeatherArb's network of 18,222 meteorological nodes. Each city receives a Z-Score — the number of standard deviations from the NASA POWER ERA5-Land 25-year baseline (2000-2024).

WWAI = Σ(critical×40 + severe×15 + moderate×5) / total_cities × 100. A score of 0 means perfectly normal global weather. A score of 100 would mean every city is in extreme anomaly — historically unprecedented.
