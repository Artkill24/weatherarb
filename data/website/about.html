WeatherArb is an independent weather intelligence platform that uses statistical analysis to identify significant deviations from historical climate norms — 24 to 72 hours before they impact local populations.

We are not traditional meteorologists. We are climate data analysts focused on translating raw atmospheric data into actionable local intelligence.

Methodology

Using the Nano-Pulse Engine, we continuously cross-reference ERA5-Land reanalysis data from the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts (ECMWF) with real-time OpenWeatherMap forecasts.

Each city receives a Z-Score — a statistical measure of how far current conditions deviate from the 2000–2024 seasonal baseline. Events scoring above Z=2.0 are classified as EXTREME; above Z=3.0 as CRITICAL.

Data Sources

ERA5-Land — Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S), ECMWF. Spatial resolution ~9km, covering 1940–present. Used for historical baseline computation.

OpenWeatherMap — Real-time observations and 72-hour forecasts. Used for current condition detection and peak prediction.

Coverage

Currently monitoring 47 provinces in Northern Italy and 8 major German cities. Expansion to Spain and France in progress. All signals are published openly via our free API.
